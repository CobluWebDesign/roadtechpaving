FAQ

Have Questions About Asphalt Sealcoating?

Sealcoating can help protect and maintain asphalt, but timing and surface condition are important. Here are answers to common questions from homeowners, business owners, and property managers.

Contact Us

  • Sealcoating adds a protective layer over the asphalt surface that helps reduce damage from sunlight, moisture, oil, and everyday wear. It also restores a darker, more uniform appearance to faded pavement.

  • Many asphalt surfaces benefit from sealcoating every two to three years, depending on age, traffic, weather exposure, and overall condition. We can inspect your pavement and recommend an appropriate maintenance schedule.

  • Curing time depends on temperature, humidity, sunlight, and the specific surface conditions. In many cases, vehicles should remain off the pavement for at least 24 to 48 hours. We'll provide specific instructions after application.

  • Sealcoating is intended to protect pavement in generally sound condition, not repair major cracks, potholes, base failure, or severely deteriorated asphalt. Damaged areas may need to be repaired before sealcoating is applied.
